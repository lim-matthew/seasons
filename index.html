why does seasons are exist?

le why?

Seasons exist in Australia due to the scientific causes related to the Earth's axial tilt and its orbit around the Sun. The Earth's axis is tilted at an angle of about 23.5 degrees relative to its orbit. This tilt is the primary reason for the existence of seasons worldwide.

During the southern hemisphere summer, which occurs from December to February in Australia, the Southern Hemisphere is tilted towards the Sun. This tilt causes the Sun's rays to strike the southern part of Australia more directly, resulting in longer daylight hours and more intense sunlight. As a result, the temperatures are generally warmer during this time.

In contrast, during the southern hemisphere winter, which spans from June to August in Australia, the Southern Hemisphere is tilted away from the Sun. This tilt causes the Sun's rays to be spread out over a larger area, resulting in shorter daylight hours and less direct sunlight. Consequently, the temperatures tend to be cooler during this period.

The transitional seasons of spring and autumn occur between summer and winter. During spring (September to November), the tilt of the Earth's axis is such that neither the northern nor the southern hemisphere is tilted towards the Sun. Similarly, during autumn (March to May), the Earth's tilt is such that neither hemisphere is tilted towards the Sun. This results in more balanced daylight hours and temperatures gradually warming up from the cooler winter period.
